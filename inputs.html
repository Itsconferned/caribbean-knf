The Foundation

Core Inputs

Master these four essential KNF inputs for optimal poultry health and performance.

FPJ

Fermented Plant Juice

Growth-promoting enzymes extracted from fast-growing plants. Rich in nutrients and beneficial microorganisms.

How to Make

  1. Harvest fast-growing plant tips (papaya, banana, sweet potato) at dawn
  2. Chop and weigh the plant material
  3. Mix with equal weight of brown sugar (1:1 ratio)
  4. Pack tightly in container, cover with breathable cloth
  5. Ferment 7-10 days in a cool, dark place
  6. Strain and store the liquid

Storage

  • Store away from sunlight
  • Keep in cool location (not refrigerated)
  • Shelf life: 6-12 months

Tips

  • Banana flower and aloe vera yield generous amounts of FPJ
  • Add a sugar cap after packing tightly to fully cover the exposed material — this prevents mold formation
LAB

Lactic Acid Bacteria

Beneficial bacteria essential for gut health, disease prevention, and improved feed conversion.

How to Make

1
Collect Rice Wash Water

The process begins by capturing wild microbes from the environment using a carbohydrate source.

  • Rinse rice: Wash a portion of rice as if you were preparing it for a meal.
  • Save the water: Collect the first rinse of the rice wash water. This initial rinse is preferred because only the strongest bacterial strains can survive in such a nutrient-poor solution.
  • Initial Fermentation: Pour the water into a shallow glass or ceramic dish (filling it to about 20 cm) and cover it with a breathable material like a paper towel or cheesecloth.
  • Environment and Timing: Place the jar in a cool, shaded area away from direct sunlight. Let it sit for 2 days.
  • The Indicator: It is ready when the water smells slightly sweet and has begun to separate. If the smell is very sour, the fermentation has gone too long and should be restarted.
2
Inoculate the Milk

Once the rice wash water is ready, you must provide the bacteria with a more nutrient-dense environment—milk—to allow them to multiply vigorously and exclude less desirable strains.

  • Proportions: Mix the clear fermented rice wash water with milk in a 1:10 ratio (one part water to ten parts milk).
  • Milk Choice: You can use any animal milk (cow, goat, sheep), whether raw, pasteurized, or even powdered nonfat milk. However, the best results come from animals not fed antibiotics. Raw milk, Nestle Proud Land 100% Local Milk or Moo Full Cream UHT Milk is preferred.
  • Fermentation: Cover the container with a breathable cover and store it in a dark, undisturbed spot at room temperature.
3
Separation of Curds and Whey

During this stage, the bacteria consume the sugars in the milk, causing the protein, fat, and starch to separate.

  • Duration: This typically takes 2 to 3 days, depending on the ambient temperature.
  • The Result: The mixture will separate into two distinct layers: solid white curds that float to the top and a pale yellow liquid (whey) at the bottom.
  • Extraction: Carefully remove the curds or push them aside and strain the clear yellow liquid into a clean jar. This liquid is your concentrated Lactic Acid Bacteria serum.
4
Storage and Maintenance

LAB serum is highly active and must be stabilized for long-term use.

  • Refrigerator Storage: The serum can be stored directly in a refrigerator for a maximum of 3 months.
  • Room Temperature Storage: To keep LAB at room temperature, mix the serum with an equal weight of brown sugar. Cover it loosely to allow the mixture to remain active and stable for up to one year.
  • Standard Dilution: For most applications on the farm, LAB is diluted at a ratio of 1:1000 (approximately 4 ml or 1 teaspoon per gallon of water).
BRV

Natural Vinegar

Essential for WCA production and gut health. Made from FPJ solids or purchased as raw apple cider vinegar.

How to Make (from FPJ Solids)

Recipe: Add 2 parts water to FPJ solids + 1 tbsp ACV (apple cider vinegar with mother). Wait 4-5 weeks.

  1. After straining FPJ, keep the leftover plant solids
  2. Add 2 parts water to 1 part solids
  3. Add 1 tablespoon raw apple cider vinegar as starter
  4. Cover with cloth, store in dark place
  5. Ferment for 4-5 weeks
  6. Test pH (should be 2.5-3.5)
  7. Strain and bottle

Storage

  • Store in glass containers
  • Shelf life: Indefinite
  • Alternative: Use raw apple cider vinegar
WCA

Water-Soluble Calcium

Essential calcium for strong eggshells and bone development. Critical for laying hens.

How to Make

  1. Collect eggshells or roast bones until chalky white
  2. Crush into small pieces
  3. Place 1 part shells in container
  4. Add 10 parts natural vinegar
  5. Cover loosely (gas will escape during reaction)
  6. Ready when bubbling completely stops (7-20 days)
  7. Strain and store the liquid

Storage

  • Do not seal tightly until bubbling stops
  • Store in cool, dark place
  • Shelf life: Indefinite if stored properly
