Nutritive Cycle Theory

Feeding Regime

A structured approach that prioritizes developing a robust digestive system early in life to ensure lifelong health, high feed efficiency, and disease resistance.

Unlike conventional methods that use soft, powdered feed, KNF utilizes tough, high-fiber inputs to "train the intestines," resulting in chickens with digestive tracts significantly longer than those raised conventionally. This allows for superior nutrient absorption throughout life.

🥚 For Layers

Phase 1

Training the Intestines Days 1–3

The very first food a chick receives determines its digestive capacity for life.

  • Feed: Raw, whole uncooked brown rice grains in unlimited quantity
  • Restriction: No other food or supplements during these first three days
  • Purpose: Forces development of thick, muscular stomach walls and extends intestine/cecum length (up to 7× longer than conventional birds)
Phase 2

Early Development Day 3 to Week 2

Nutritional complexity is added to mimic "mother's milk" and introduce fiber.

  • Hard-Boiled Egg Yolks: Sieve or mash and smear onto a hard surface (cardboard or wooden board) to prevent choking
  • Fibrous Leaves: Fresh, finely chopped grass (bamboo, corn leaves, elephant grass, bull grass) — as much as they can consume in 5 minutes
  • Water Placement: Position probiotic chick water at the opposite end of the brooding box to encourage 50–60 daily exercise runs
Phase 3

Transition and Growth Weeks 2–8

The feeding schedule shifts to a structured rhythm.

  • "Once Full, Once Hungry" Rule: Feed only once per day at 10:00 AM or 2 hours before sunset (~4:00 PM)
  • Feed: Begin mixing in fermented chick starter
  • Supplementation: Continue with grass cuttings and add non-toxic fruit/vegetable scraps
  • Avoid: Nightshade parts (green potatoes, tomato/eggplant leaves), avocado, apple seeds, stone fruit pits, raw/dried beans, large amounts of onions/garlic
Phase 4

Intermediate Phase Weeks 8–16

  • Feed: Fermented grower feed
  • Continue supplementation with scraps and grass cuttings
Phase 5

Adulthood Week 16+

  • Feed: Fermented layer feed
  • Supplements: Calcium Supplement and/or Fermented Sea Water
  • Living Floor: Chickens will consume fermented manure and microorganisms from the deep litter (~10% of diet)
  • Result: Birds remain productive for years longer than conventionally raised poultry (approximately 2-2.5 years)

🍗 For Broilers

Broilers are fed as much as they can eat of the KNF mixture to reach market weight efficiently.

Phase 1

Intestinal Training Day 1

The first day is the most critical for determining lifetime feed efficiency.

  • Feed: Raw, whole uncooked brown rice grains in unlimited quantity
Phase 2

Early Development Day 2 to Week 2

The diet expands to include protein and fiber to mimic natural nourishment.

  • Feed: Fermented starter feed
  • Fibrous Leaves: Fresh, finely chopped grass (bamboo, corn leaves, elephant grass, bull grass) — as much as consumed in 5 minutes
  • Exercise: Position probiotic chick water at opposite end of brooding area to encourage 50–60 daily runs
Phase 3

Transition and Growth Weeks 2 to Day 40

The bird's metabolism is ready for a complex diet and structured rhythm.

  • Schedule: Feed once per day, ~2 hours before sundown (~4:00 PM)
  • Principle: "Once full, once hungry" to boost immunity
  • Feed: Fermented Grower Feed
Phase 4

Market Weight Drive Day 40 to Slaughter

Adult broilers thrive on locally sourced materials and fermented waste products.

  • Feed: Fermented finisher feed
  • Supplementation: Continue with non-toxic fruit/vegetable scraps and grass cuttings
